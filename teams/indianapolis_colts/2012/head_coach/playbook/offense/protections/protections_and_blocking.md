## Playbook protection/blocking extract

## 5. BLOCKING & PROTECTION SCHEMES

**Pass Protection Systems:**  
Six-Man Protection (RB + 5 OL): Standard for balanced pass  
Seven-Man Protection (RB + TE + 5 OL): For chunk passes  
Big-On-Big Protection: Man on biggest threats  
Max Protection (8-9 blockers): For deep shots  
Slide Protection (Left/Right): Directional slide vs blitz  
BOB Protection (Big-On-Big): Priority threats  
Man Protection: Individual assignments  
Zone Protection: Area coverage  
Combo Protection: Mixed man/zone  
Half-Slide Protection: Half man, half slide for mobility  

**Run Blocking Schemes:**  
Inside Zone Blocking: Horizontal stretch  
Outside Zone Blocking: Edge stretch  
Gap Scheme Blocking: Gap focus  
Power Blocking: Pull and lead  
Counter Blocking: Misdirection pulls  
Trap Blocking: Trap on threat  
Pull Schemes: Leverage pulls  
Reach Blocking: Reach seal  
Combo Blocks: Double team climb  
Base Blocks: Base drive

## Game-Day pressure/protection extract

### Pressure Recognition & Response

**Defensive Front Alignment:**
- **4-man front (4-3 base):** Even front, DTs on guards
- **3-man front (3-4 base):** Odd front, NT on center, DEs outside
- **Nickel front:** Usually 4 down, lighter/faster
- **Dime front:** Usually 4 down, very light

**Pressure Indicators:**
- DBs in press coverage
- Safeties closer to LOS
- Linebackers showing in gaps
- Edge rushers in two-point stance
- Defensive linemen wider splits (easier to rush)

**Blitz Types & Responses:**

**Standard Rush (4 rushers):**
- **Response:** 6-man protection standard
- **Concepts:** All pass game available
- **Timing:** Normal timing applies

**Fire Zone (5 rushers):**
- **Identification:** One LB or DB comes, someone drops
- **Response:** 6-man protection can handle
- **Hot route:** If unblocked rusher
- **Attack:** Area where they dropped from

**Blitz (6+ rushers):**
- **Identification:** Multiple LBs/DBs coming
- **Response:** 
  - Max protection (7-8 blockers)
  - Or hot routes/quick game
  - Or screen game
- **Attack:** Vacated areas behind blitz

**Sim Pressure (Show blitz, rush 4):**
- **Identification:** Multiple players showing, but drop out
- **Response:** Don't panic, execute play
- **Read:** Post-snap drops, not pre-snap show

**Zero Blitz (No deep safety, all come):**
- **Identification:** All DBs press, usually all come
- **Response:**
  - Hot routes immediately
  - Max protection rarely helps
  - Get ball out in under 2.5 seconds
  - Slants work great
- **Attack:** DBs on islands, no help over top

**Protection Adjustments:**
- **6-man:** Standard (5 OL + 1 RB)
- **7-man:** 5 OL + RB + TE
- **8-man:** 5 OL + RB + both TEs
- **Max (9-man):** 5 OL + RB + 2 TE + 2 WR stay in
- **Slide Left:** OL slides protection left
- **Slide Right:** OL slides protection right
- **BOB:** Big-on-big, each blocker has man
- **Half-Slide:** Half slide, half man

**Hot Routes vs. Pressure:**
- **Slant:** Most common hot route
- **Hitch:** Quick sit down
- **Spot:** Quick breaking route
- **Fade:** If CB pressed and blitzing
- **RB Angle:** RB out of backfield
