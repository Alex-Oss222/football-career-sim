## Game-Day LOS management

### Offensive Line of Scrimmage Management

**Getting to Line of Scrimmage:**
- Efficient substitutions
- Hustle to avoid delay
- Snap before play clock under 5 if possible

**Pre-Snap Process:**
- QB identifies mike
- QB scans defense
- QB checks coverage
- QB looks for blitz
- Motion if applicable
- Audible if needed
- Snap ball

**Tempo Variations:**

**Normal Tempo:**
- Snap 20-15 seconds on play clock
- Allows defensive substitutions
- Allows audibles
- Standard approach

**Up-Tempo:**
- Snap 25-20 seconds on play clock
- Limits defensive substitutions
- Limited audibles
- Tires defense

**Hurry-Up:**
- Snap immediately after ready for play
- No defensive substitutions possible
- No audibles
- Maximum pressure on defense

**When to Use Tempo:**
- Trailing in 4th quarter
- Defense substituting slowly
- Defense tired
- Want to catch defense in wrong personnel
- Two-minute situations
- After big play to keep defense off balance

## Game-Day audibles / pre-snap adjustments

### Audibles & Pre-Snap Adjustments

**Audible System:**

**Primary Play Called:**
- Called in huddle or signaled no-huddle
- Has built-in checks based on defensive look

**Kill Calls:**
- Completely kill play, go to new play
- **When:** Defense shows something unexpected
- **Process:** Signal kill, call new play at LOS

**Check-With-Me:**
- Two plays called, QB chooses at LOS
- **When:** Uncertain of defensive look
- **Process:** Call both plays, QB decides based on defense

**Built-In Checks:**
- Play has automatic adjustments
- **Example:** RPO has built-in pass option if box loaded
- **Process:** QB reads and executes built-in adjustment

**Hot Routes:**
- Adjust route vs. pressure
- **When:** Unblocked rusher identified
- **Process:** Signal to receiver, throw hot

**Protection Adjustments:**
- Change protection scheme at LOS
- **When:** Different pressure than expected
- **Process:** Call protection adjustment

**Formation Adjustment:**
- Change formation at LOS
- **When:** Better formation needed vs. defense shown
- **Process:** Signal formation change

**Snap Count Variation:**
- Change when ball snapped
- **Purpose:** Catch defense off-guard
- **Process:** Call different snap count at LOS

**Audible Triggers:**
- **Box Count:** 8+ in box on run play → check to pass
- **Coverage:** Wrong coverage for play called → check
- **Pressure:** Blitz coming → adjust protection or play
- **Matchup:** Bad matchup → change route or formation
- **Leverage:** Wrong leverage → adjust route concept

## Game-Day communication protocol

### Communication Protocol

**Play-Call Process:**

1. **Situation Assessment (3-5 seconds after previous play):**
   - Down and distance
   - Field position
   - Hash mark
   - Score
   - Time remaining
   - Defensive personnel

2. **Play Selection (5-10 seconds):**
   - Reference call sheet
   - Consider tendencies
   - Trust gut feel
   - Make decision

3. **Communication (10-15 seconds):**
   - Signal to QB (no-huddle)
   - Or wait for QB to come to sideline (huddle)
   - Communicate play, adjustments, alerts

4. **QB Execution (15-40 seconds):**
   - QB communicates in huddle or at LOS
   - Pre-snap process
   - Audibles if needed
   - Execute play

**Sideline Communication:**
- With QB: Constant dialogue about what he's seeing
- With OC staff: Position coaches provide input
- With upstairs: Press box coaches provide info on defensive adjustments
- With analytics: Situational recommendations

**Information Flow:**

**From Press Box:**
- Defensive formation/coverage identification
- Defensive tendency alerts
- Personnel matchup observations
- Recommended concepts

**To Press Box:**
- What play was called
- What happened (defense's response)
- What to scout for next series

**Timeout Usage:**
- To avoid delay of game
- To set up critical play
- To ice opposing kicker
- To preserve time (end of half/game)
- Strategic (before 4th down, before crucial 3rd down)
