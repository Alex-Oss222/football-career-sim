### Motion Usage & Pre-Snap Adjustments

**Types of Motion:**

**Jet Motion:**
- WR runs full speed across formation
- **Purpose:**
  - Identify man vs. zone (does DB follow?)
  - Create numbers advantage (move defender)
  - Fake jet sweep
  - Motion into better alignment
- **When to use:** Every formation, all situations

**Orbit Motion:**
- WR or RB arcs from one side to other
- **Purpose:**
  - Same as jet but slower
  - Better for reading defensive adjustments
  - Can fake orbit sweep
  - Can motion into backfield

**Return/Shuffle Motion:**
- Player motions across then returns to original spot
- **Purpose:**
  - Pure coverage identification
  - Force defensive communication
  - See how defense handles motion
  - No handoff threat

**TE Trade:**
- TE motions from one side of formation to other
- **Purpose:**
  - Change formation strength
  - Force front adjustment
  - Identify where 3-tech moves
  - Create different run gaps

**RB Motion:**
- RB motions from backfield to slot
- **Purpose:**
  - Create passing matchup (LB on RB in space)
  - Empty then motion back (force communication)
  - Identify box count changes
  - Protection adjustments

**Pre-Snap Shifts:**
- Multiple players change positions
- **Purpose:**
  - Force defensive communication
  - Create confusion
  - Get into better formation
  - Time snap for advantage

**Motion Timing:**
- **Early Motion (12-15 seconds):** Get into formation
- **Medium Motion (8-10 seconds):** Force adjustments
- **Late Motion (5-7 seconds):** Coverage identification
- **Snap-at-motion:** Jet sweep threat, defensive confusion

**Reading Defensive Response:**
- **No one moves:** Zone coverage likely
- **One player follows:** Man coverage confirmed
- **Multiple adjust:** Zone with some man principles
- **Front adjusts:** Note where 3-tech goes, where LBs fit
- **Safeties rotate:** Note rotation direction and timing
- **Late rotation:** They're disguising, read post-snap

**Using Motion Every Play:**
- Use motion 85-90% of snaps in Q1 for information
- Continue using 70-80% throughout game
- Forces defense to communicate every play
- Creates mistakes when defense tired
- Provides consistent coverage information

### Pressure Recognition & Response

**Defensive Front Alignment:**
- **4-man front (4-3 base):** Even front, DTs on guards
- **3-man front (3-4 base):** Odd front, NT on center, DEs outside
- **Nickel front:** Usually 4 down, lighter/faster
- **Dime front:** Usually 4 down, very light

**Pressure Indicators:**
- DBs in press coverage
- Safeties closer to LOS
- Linebackers showing in gaps
- Edge rushers in two-point stance
- Defensive linemen wider splits (easier to rush)

**Blitz Types & Responses:**

**Standard Rush (4 rushers):**
- **Response:** 6-man protection standard
- **Concepts:** All pass game available
- **Timing:** Normal timing applies

**Fire Zone (5 rushers):**
- **Identification:** One LB or DB comes, someone drops
- **Response:** 6-man protection can handle
- **Hot route:** If unblocked rusher
- **Attack:** Area where they dropped from

**Blitz (6+ rushers):**
- **Identification:** Multiple LBs/DBs coming
- **Response:** 
  - Max protection (7-8 blockers)
  - Or hot routes/quick game
  - Or screen game
- **Attack:** Vacated areas behind blitz

**Sim Pressure (Show blitz, rush 4):**
- **Identification:** Multiple players showing, but drop out
- **Response:** Don't panic, execute play
- **Read:** Post-snap drops, not pre-snap show

**Zero Blitz (No deep safety, all come):**
- **Identification:** All DBs press, usually all come
- **Response:**
  - Hot routes immediately
  - Max protection rarely helps
  - Get ball out in under 2.5 seconds
  - Slants work great
- **Attack:** DBs on islands, no help over top

**Protection Adjustments:**
- **6-man:** Standard (5 OL + 1 RB)
- **7-man:** 5 OL + RB + TE
- **8-man:** 5 OL + RB + both TEs
- **Max (9-man):** 5 OL + RB + 2 TE + 2 WR stay in
- **Slide Left:** OL slides protection left
- **Slide Right:** OL slides protection right
- **BOB:** Big-on-big, each blocker has man
- **Half-Slide:** Half slide, half man

**Hot Routes vs. Pressure:**
- **Slant:** Most common hot route
- **Hitch:** Quick sit down
- **Spot:** Quick breaking route
- **Fade:** If CB pressed and blitzing
- **RB Angle:** RB out of backfield
