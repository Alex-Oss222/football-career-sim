## WHAT THE 2000 EAGLES OFFENSE IS

Reid's Walsh/Holmgren West Coast Offense, entering Year 2 of installation. The foundation was laid in 1999 — terminology, core concepts, timing, rhythm. The 8-8 record proved the base system works. Year 2 expands it. The playbook roughly doubles. This is standard for WCO installations: Year 1 is the base, Year 2 is the layers.

The 2000 offense should look schematically advanced compared to 1999 — more pre-snap complexity, more route adjustments, more situational depth. It should be harder to defend because there is more to prepare for.

McNabb is the unquestioned starter entering his second year. The offense is being tailored to his strengths — his mobility, arm strength, ability to extend plays — while continuing to develop him as a full WCO passer. McNabb's development is the organizational priority. Everything on the offensive side serves this.

## WCO CORE PRINCIPLES — UNCHANGED

All six core principles from the Year 1 document remain:

1. **The pass sets up the run.** Short, high-percentage passes function like run plays. The run exploits soft boxes created by pass respect.
2. **Timing and rhythm.** Passes thrown on time to a spot. 3-step, 5-step, 7-step drops synced with route depths.
3. **Progression reads.** Defined decision trees for every concept. QB reads defenders in sequence.
4. **Horizontal stretches.** Route combinations put multiple receivers on one defender.
5. **Personnel diversity.** Multiple formations and personnel groupings create pre-snap confusion.
6. **Ball control through the air.** Completed short passes = run game efficiency.

## YEAR 2 EXPANSION AREAS

### 1. Expanded Formation Menu

**Year 1 formations (base — still in use):** Doubles, Trips, Spread, Near, Far, I-Formation.

**Year 2 additions:**

- **Empty:** No back. Five receivers. Forces defense to declare coverage pre-snap. Requires precise protection communication — no back to pick up free rushers. The QB handles blitz-identification responsibility.
- **Bunch:** Three receivers aligned tightly on one side. Creates pick routes, rub routes, and high-traffic areas that exploit man coverage specifically.
- **Stack:** Two receivers aligned vertically. Creates free releases and coordinated route options based on inside/outside declarations.
- **Heavy / Jumbo:** Extra OL or TE personnel for short-yardage and goalline. Disguises run/pass.
- **Compressed / reduced splits:** Receivers closer to the formation. Changes route timing and defensive leverage.
- **Formation tags:** Modifier words that alter base formations — flip, over, empty, bunch, stack. Use the same play-call syntax (formation + personnel + motion + protection + concept).

**TE formation impact in Year 2:** TEs move more. More trades (switching sides between plays), more motions (pre-snap movement), more flex alignments (TE split wide or in the slot). The same play call gives the TE different assignments depending on the formation tag.

### 2. Option Routes and Sight Adjustments — Expanded

**Year 1:** Basic option routes. One defender read, two route variations based on leverage.

**Year 2:**

- **Multi-read option routes:** Receiver reads two defenders (or defender + coverage structure) and chooses from three route variations. Example: TE reads the Will linebacker — if Will drops to hook zone, break flat at 5; if Will walls the flat, bend to dig at 12; if Will blitzes, sit in void at 8.
- **Expanded sight adjustments:** More blitz patterns trigger more hot-route conversions. The TE and slot are the primary sight-adjustment receivers.
- **Post-snap option routes:** Year 1 options were mostly pre-snap. Year 2 adds mid-route adjustments based on coverage reads after the snap.

All option routes are rules-based, not freelance. Every option has a defined decision tree.

### 3. Play-Action Expansion

**Year 1:** Basic play-action — fake run, throw pass. Simple boots and nakeds.

**Year 2:**

- **Boot / naked / waggle:** Three rollout actions, each with designed route concepts. Different run fakes trigger different boot concepts. Year 2 expands the menu significantly.
- **Deep play-action shots:** Run fake holds safeties; deep route gets behind them. Integrated with the expanded route tree.
- **Screen game off play-action:** Fake PA, throw screen. Attacks defenses that crash on the run fake.
- **Play-action + motion combinations:** Motion pre-snap, run fake, throw off the motion action. Layered deception.

The TE seam route off play-action remains one of the WCO's most dangerous plays. Year 2 expands the TE's PA route menu and adds more blocking/release variations.

### 4. Situational Football Packages

**Year 1:** Basic red zone and third-down packages. Limited two-minute. Minimal goalline.

**Year 2:**

- **Red zone (inside 20):** Full package. Specific concepts for inside-the-20, inside-the-10, and goalline. Route depths compress. The TE is critical in red-zone WCO concepts.
- **Third-down by distance:** 3rd-short (1-3), 3rd-medium (4-7), 3rd-long (8+). Each bucket has specific route concepts and protection calls.
- **Two-minute / hurry-up:** Structured package — limited play menu, communication shortcuts, tempo management.
- **Backed-up (inside own 10):** Conservative. Limited formations, high-percentage throws, avoid turnovers.
- **Four-minute / clock management:** Run-heavy look with PA constraints when protecting a lead.

### 5. Protection Scheme Expansion

**Year 1:** Slide, Jet, Max, Half-slide.

**Year 2:**

- **Blitz-specific protections:** Calls designed for specific blitz patterns. Protection changes based on defensive front and pre-snap read.
- **TE protection versatility:** Block, chip-and-release, or immediate release — depends on protection call, formation, and blitz ID. More variable than Year 1.
- **Expanded hot route menu:** When protection breaks down, QB and designated receiver (often TE) convert to hot. More blitz patterns mapped to more hot options.

## PASSING STRUCTURE — YEAR 2 UPDATES

### Quick Game (3-step)

- Same purpose: fast ball, chain-moving.
- **Year 2:** More concepts from new formations (bunch, empty). Expanded screen game (bubble, tunnel). Larger percentage of the passing attack as McNabb's timing sharpens.

### Intermediate (5-step)

- Same purpose: attack the middle, horizontal stretches.
- **Year 2:** Expanded option routes at intermediate depth. 5-step concepts now carry 3-4 read progressions instead of 2-3. TE often the second or third read, running option routes that adjust by coverage.

### Deep / Play-Action (7-step, PA)

- Same purpose: attack deep, exploit run-defense aggression.
- **Year 2:** More PA varieties. Deep shots integrated with the run game — when the run works, the deep PA becomes explosive. TE seam off PA is a Year 2 emphasis.

## HOW TEs ARE USED IN REID'S WCO — YEAR 2

**Y (Inline TE) — Year 2:**
- Full route tree from inline. Year 2 adds double-moves, option routes, and delayed releases off PA.
- Blocking assignments more variable — protection call determines block, chip, or release.
- More motion and trade usage.

**H (Move TE / Slot TE) — Year 2:**
- Bigger weapon in 12 personnel. Aligned slot, wing, or flexed — creates mismatches (too big for DBs, too fast for LBs).
- Multi-read option routes from the H are a Year 2 emphasis.
- Primary red-zone target from the slot.

**Year 2 TE complexity:** More formation variety = more alignment changes. More option routes = more reads. More protection variety = more blocking-assignment variation. The TE must know the full playbook — not just his own assignments, but how they change by formation, protection, and coverage.

## WCO TERMINOLOGY — YEAR 2 STATUS

### Unchanged from Year 1

- **Play-call syntax:** Formation — Personnel — Motion — Protection — Concept. Same structure.
- **Position labels:** X, Z, Y, H, F, T. Same designations.
- **Route numbering:** 0-9 route tree. Same convention.
- **Drop designations:** 3-step, 5-step, 7-step. Same mechanics.

### Year 2 Additions

- **Formation tags:** Flip, over, empty, bunch, stack — modifiers appended to base formation names.
- **Option route call language:** Tags within the play call that indicate option-route rules are active for a specific receiver.
- **Situational play-call sheet organization:** Game-day card now organized by situation (red zone, third-down, two-minute) in addition to personnel and concept.
- **Boot/naked/waggle terminology:** Specific call words for each rollout action and the route concepts attached.

The vocabulary grows but the grammar stays the same. New concepts use the existing syntax.
