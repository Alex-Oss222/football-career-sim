## WHAT'S REALISTIC AT EACH LEVEL

| Level | Realistic? | Explanation |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Head Coach (any level) | **NO** | Requires 10+ years coaching experience and coordinator tenure. Not accessible with two years of QC. |
| Offensive Coordinator | **NO** | Requires 5+ years position-coach experience, play-calling reps, scheme-design authority. |
| Position Coach (Power Conference) | **UNLIKELY** | Deep candidate pools. Two-year NFL QC coaches compete against candidates with 5-10 years of college position-coaching and recruiting track records. Possible only with a personal connection to the HC or a program that specifically values NFL credibility over recruiting experience. |
| Position Coach (Mid-Major / Group of 5) | **YES** | Thinner candidate pools, lower budgets, high value placed on NFL name recognition for recruiting. A HOF TE with two years of NFL QC under Andy Reid is competitive for TE coach or offensive assistant roles. |
| GA/QC (Any college) | **YES — OVERQUALIFIED** | Available anywhere as a fallback. Not advisable — lateral or backward move from NFL QC. |

### USC — OFFENSIVE STAFF POSITIONS

**Program:** USC Trojans
**Conference:** Pac-10
**New HC:** Pete Carroll
**HC Background:** NFL head coach (NY Jets 1994, New England Patriots 1997-99). Fired by Patriots after going 27-21. Defensive background but installs pro-style offense.
**Staff Build Status:** FROM SCRATCH — Hackett fired, Carroll building entirely new staff.
**System:** Pro-style offense with NFL concepts. Carroll bringing NFL assistants and college coaches.

**Openings:** Multiple offensive position-coach roles as Carroll builds from scratch. TE coach, WR coach, and offensive assistant positions available.

**Competition:** HIGH — USC is a premier program. Carroll's NFL connections give him a deep candidate pool. Los Angeles is a destination city.

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Coaching experience (college or NFL)
- Recruiting ability in Southern California — the most talent-rich region in the country
- System installation competence
- Carroll values energy, competitiveness, and positivity in his coaching culture

**Attraction Score: 4/10**
- Carroll has NFL connections filling offensive roles. High competition.
- Pro-style system is relevant to NFL-trained coaches.
- No personal connection is the primary barrier.
- USC's recruiting territory (SoCal) requires established high school relationships that a two-year NFL QC doesn't have.

**Organizational Notes:**
- Carroll transforms USC into a dynasty (winning two national championships). Getting in early with Carroll's staff has enormous long-term career value — but nobody knows that in January 2001.
- USC's recruiting machine requires coaches who can sell. Recruiting experience (REC) matters more here than scheme knowledge.

### OHIO STATE — OFFENSIVE STAFF POSITIONS

**Program:** Ohio State Buckeyes
**Conference:** Big Ten
**New HC:** Jim Tressel
**HC Background:** Youngstown State HC (1991-2000). Four Division I-AA national championships. Conservative, pro-style, ground-game emphasis.
**Staff Build Status:** FROM SCRATCH — Cooper fired, Tressel building new staff with a mix of Youngstown State assistants and Ohio high school/college connections.
**System:** Pro-style, run-heavy, physical offense. Tressel values ball security, field position, and defense.

**Openings:** Multiple offensive position-coach roles.

**Competition:** VERY HIGH — Ohio State is a top-5 program nationally. Every opening attracts dozens of qualified candidates.

**Attraction Score: 3/10**
- Tressel brings his own people from Youngstown State and the Ohio coaching network.
- Tressel's conservative offensive philosophy doesn't align with WCO-trained coaches.
- No personal connection.
- Ohio State's recruiting territory (Ohio, Midwest) requires established regional relationships.

**Program:** Maryland Terrapins
**Conference:** ACC
**New HC:** Ralph Friedgen
**HC Background:** Georgia Tech OC. Former Maryland player. Returning to his alma mater after a successful career as one of the best OCs in college football.
**Staff Build Status:** FROM SCRATCH — Vanderlinden fired after 12-33. Friedgen rebuilding completely.

**Openings:** Multiple offensive position-coach roles. Friedgen needs an entire offensive staff.

**Competition:** MODERATE — Maryland is a lower-tier ACC program. Candidate pool is thinner than at USC or Ohio State. Friedgen needs to recruit coaches, not just select from an overflow of applicants.

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Coaching experience (lower bar than premier programs)

- Recruiting ability in the DMV (DC/Maryland/Virginia) corridor
- Willingness to rebuild a bad program (12-33 under predecessor)

**Attraction Score: 5/10**
- Lower-tier ACC program = thinner candidate pool, more accessible.
- Friedgen is an offensive coach who might value an NFL-experienced TE coach.
- Rebuilding program = lower stakes for a position coach learning on the job.
- No personal connection is the main barrier.
- DMV recruiting corridor is competitive — established high school relationships matter.

**Salary Range:** $60,000–$100,000 (ACC position coach)

### UNIVERSITY OF MIAMI — OFFENSIVE STAFF

**Program:** Miami Hurricanes
**Conference:** Big East
**New HC:** Larry Coker (promoted from OC after Butch Davis left for Cleveland Browns)
**HC Background:** First-time HC. Was Davis's OC. Inheriting a national championship-caliber roster.
**Staff Build Status:** CONTINUITY — Coker keeping most of Davis's staff intact. Selective changes, not a full overhaul.
**System:** Pro-style offense (Davis/Coker system). Power run, play-action, pro concepts. NFL-ready scheme.

**Openings:** LIMITED — Coker retaining most staff. Position-coach openings only if a specific assistant leaves (follows Davis to Cleveland, takes another job, or is let go).

**Competition:** MODERATE (if an opening exists) — Miami is a top-10 program with a stacked roster (Ken Dorsey at QB, Clinton Portis at RB, Andre Johnson at WR, Jeremy Shockey at TE). Quality candidates want to be here. But Coker's continuity approach means fewer openings.

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Coaching experience
- Recruiting ability in South Florida — the richest talent pipeline in the country
- Program familiarity preferred (Miami culture is specific and intense)
- Ability to coach elite-level talent

**Attraction Score: 6/10**
- Stone is a Miami alum and HOF player. The connection (relationship score 55, dormant) provides a warm entry point that no other candidate can match.
- Recruiting value is VERY HIGH — a HOF Miami TE on the coaching staff is a direct pipeline to South Florida high school recruits. Stone's name carries enormous weight with players and families in Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach counties.
- BUT: Coker is keeping most of Davis's staff. Openings are limited. If the TE coach or an offensive assistant position doesn't open, there's no slot for Stone regardless of connection.
- Stone would need to proactively activate the dormant Miami connection — reach out to Coker or remaining contacts in the program.
- Jeremy Shockey at TE (entering his sophomore year) is a future NFL first-round pick. Coaching Shockey would be a high-profile, high-stakes development opportunity.

**Salary Range:** $60,000–$100,000 (Big East position coach at a top program)

**Key Considerations:**
- Miami wins the 2001 national championship. Being on this staff is career gold — but nobody knows that yet.
- Coker is a first-time HC inheriting a loaded roster. He's conservative about staff changes.
- The Miami recruiting advantage is the strongest argument for this opening — Stone's HOF status in South Florida is worth more here than anywhere else in college football.
- The dormant connection needs activation. Passive waiting won't work.

### CONFERENCE USA — TE COACH / OFFENSIVE ASSISTANT (VARIOUS PROGRAMS)

**Programs:** UAB, East Carolina, Tulane, Southern Miss, Houston, Louisville (pre-Big East move), Cincinnati, and others with normal staff turnover.
**Level:** Position Coach (TE Coach, Offensive Assistant, Passing Game Coordinator)
**Salary Range:** $40,000–$80,000
**Competition:** LOW — thin candidate pools. Programs actively recruit coaches.

**Why these openings exist:** Every offseason, Conference USA programs lose 3-5 position coaches per school to promotions, lateral moves, or HC changes at other programs. Normal carousel churn creates a constant supply of openings. New HC hires at individual programs create from-scratch staff builds with every role available.

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Football coaching experience at some level
- Recruiting ability (this is 50%+ of the job at mid-major)
- Position expertise
- Teaching ability with developmental players (Conference USA talent is below Power Conference)
- Willingness to work for below-market pay with limited resources

**Attraction Score: 7-8/10**
- A HOF NFL TE as TE coach at a Conference USA program would be headline news for the school.
- Recruiting value is enormous — high school players want to be coached by a Pro Football Hall of Famer.
- Candidate pool is thin. Two years of NFL QC under Andy Reid exceeds the experience bar.
- Resources are limited — facilities, travel budget, support staff are a fraction of Power Conference.
- The football quality is significantly below NFL or Power Conference levels.
- Room ownership and meeting command experience — the exact skills missing for NFL Level 1 — would be gained immediately.

### MAC — TE COACH / OFFENSIVE ASSISTANT (VARIOUS PROGRAMS)

**Programs:** Bowling Green, Toledo, Miami (OH), Ohio, Kent State, Akron, Ball State, and others.
**Level:** Position Coach
**Salary Range:** $35,000–$65,000
**Competition:** LOW — MAC programs have the thinnest candidate pools in FBS.

**Attraction Score: 8-9/10**
- A HOF player taking a MAC position-coach job would be the biggest coaching hire in the conference.
- Instant program credibility. Recruiting advantage is massive relative to conference peers.
- The bar for coaching experience is the lowest in FBS. Two years of NFL QC is more than adequate.
- Resources are minimal. Locations are often small Midwest towns. Facilities are basic.
- Players are developmental — the teaching experience is valuable but the football quality is far below NFL.
- Bowling Green is notable: Urban Meyer was HC from 2001-02 before going to Utah and then Ohio State. Staff openings under new HCs create opportunities.

**Programs:** WAC: Boise State, Fresno State, Hawaii, UTEP, San Jose State, Louisiana Tech, Nevada, Rice. Sun Belt: North Texas, New Mexico State, Middle Tennessee, Arkansas State, Idaho.
**Level:** Position Coach
**Salary Range:** $30,000–$60,000
**Competition:** LOW — remote locations, low budgets, thin pools.

**Attraction Score: 8-9/10**

- Boise State under Dan Hawkins is beginning to build what becomes one of the great mid-major programs — early entry has long-term value.

- Fresno State under Pat Hill is competitive and plays a physical, NFL-style brand of football.

## COLLEGE VS. NFL — KEY DIFFERENCES

| Factor | NFL | College |
|--------|-----|---------|
| Primary job beyond coaching | Film/scheme prep | **Recruiting** (50%+ of the job) |
| Player skill level | Elite professionals | 18-22 year olds, developmental |
| Compliance | CBA, salary cap | **NCAA rules** (violations = career death) |
| Schedule | Sept–Jan | Aug–Jan + recruiting year-round |
| Staff hierarchy | Rigid | More fluid, coaches wear multiple hats |
| Title accessibility | Gatekept by experience | **More accessible** at lower levels |
| Career transfer back to NFL | N/A | **Difficult** — many college coaches can't return |
| Recruiting experience (REC) | Low importance | **Critical** — the single most important skill |
| Media handling (MED) | Market-dependent | Lower at mid-major, higher at Power Conference |
