## NFL OPENINGS

### BUFFALO BILLS

**Why jobs are open:** Wade Phillips fired after the 2000 season (8-8). Gregg Williams hired from Tennessee Titans (defensive coordinator).

**Organization context:** Ralph Wilson ownership. The Bills went 8-8 in 2000 after an 11-5 playoff season in 1999. Phillips was fired partly because Wilson blamed him for the Music City Miracle debacle and subsequent decline. The roster has pieces (Eric Moulds, Peerless Price at WR) but the QB situation is unsettled — the Flutie/Johnson controversy destroyed the locker room. Williams is an aggressive, blitz-heavy defensive mind.

**Program pressure:** Moderate. Buffalo expects competitiveness. Williams gets some time because the roster needs a culture reset, but Wilson is impatient.

**Staff context:** Complete staff overhaul. Williams bringing his own people, primarily from his Tennessee DC tenure. Offensive staff is wide open — Williams is a defensive coach with no offensive network. OC hire will determine the offensive system.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $30–45K
- Competition: Low-Moderate
- Why it's open: Full staff overhaul under Williams. Every offensive role is available.
- System: TBD — depends on OC hire. Williams's Tennessee background suggests a run-heavy, play-action approach.
- What the QC would do: Standard film breakdown, scout team, administrative support for whatever system the OC installs.

---

**Opening: Tight Ends Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $125–200K
- Competition: Low-Moderate
- Why it's open: Full staff overhaul.
- Key personnel: Jay Riemersma (TE — solid starter, not a star). TE room is functional but unspectacular.
- What the TE coach would do: Run the TE room. Meetings, practice plans, technique work, film grading, game-plan installation for TE-specific assignments.

### CLEVELAND BROWNS

**Why jobs are open:** Chris Palmer fired after the 2000 season (3-13, 5-27 overall in two years). Butch Davis hired from University of Miami (HC).

**Organization context:** Expansion team entering Year 3. Palmer went 2-14 and 3-13 — the expansion patience is exhausted. Davis is a high-profile college hire who rebuilt Miami into a national championship contender. Carmen Policy and Dwight Clark in the front office. The roster is still thin but the 2001 draft (No. 3 pick) adds talent. Tim Couch at QB is struggling behind a bad offensive line.

**Program pressure:** Moderate. Cleveland is rabid for football but understands the rebuild. Davis gets time, but Year 1 improvement is expected. The fanbase wants visible progress.

**Staff context:** Complete staff overhaul. Davis bringing a mix of his Miami college assistants and NFL hires. Needs coaches at every level. The staff will blend college energy with NFL experience.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $30–45K
- Competition: Low
- Why it's open: Complete staff overhaul. Every position available.
- System: Pro-style offense (Davis's Miami background — power run, play-action, pro concepts).
- What the QC would do: Standard QC duties. On a rebuilding team, QC coaches often absorb expanded responsibilities due to thin staffing.

---

**Opening: Tight Ends Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $100–175K
- Competition: Low
- Why it's open: Full staff overhaul. No incumbent.
- Key personnel: TE room is thin — expansion-level talent. Development project.
- What the TE coach would do: Build and develop the TE room from limited talent. Heavy teaching role with young, unproven players. System installation from scratch.
- Notes: Davis coached at University of Miami. Any coaching candidate with Miami connections has a built-in advantage.

### DETROIT LIONS

**Why jobs are open:** Bobby Ross resigned mid-season in 2000. Gary Moeller served as interim HC. Marty Mornhinweg hired from San Francisco 49ers (offensive coordinator under Steve Mariucci).

**Organization context:** Matt Millen hired as GM — a former player and TV analyst with zero front-office experience. The Ford family ownership is passive and frequently makes poor hiring decisions. The Lions went 9-7 in 2000 but Ross resigned amid organizational dysfunction. Mornhinweg is a Walsh/Mariucci coaching tree product. The roster has some talent (Barry Sanders retired in 1999, but Johnnie Morton, Germane Crowell at WR; Charlie Batch/Ty Detmer at QB).

**Program pressure:** Moderate. Lions fans have low expectations but want competence. Millen as GM creates immediate structural concerns. Mornhinweg gets time but the franchise is unstable.

**Staff context:** Full staff overhaul. Mornhinweg bringing 49ers/Walsh tree coaches. Offensive staff is his domain — WCO installation. Defensive staff needs building from outside his network.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $30–45K
- Competition: Low-Moderate
- System: West Coast Offense (Walsh/Mariucci lineage).
- Why it's open: Full staff overhaul under Mornhinweg.

---

**Opening: Tight Ends Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $125–200K
- Competition: Moderate
- Key personnel: David Sloan (TE — unremarkable). TE room needs development.
- Notes: Mornhinweg's WCO background means he may prefer coaches from the Walsh/Mariucci tree for offensive positions.

### KANSAS CITY CHIEFS

**Why jobs are open:** Gunther Cunningham fired after the 2000 season (7-9). Dick Vermeil hired from retirement — former HC of St. Louis Rams (won Super Bowl XXXIV) and Philadelphia Eagles (1976-82).

**Organization context:** Lamar Hunt ownership — stable, patient, well-resourced franchise. The Chiefs went 9-7 in 1999 and 7-9 in 2000 under Cunningham, who was promoted internally after Marty Schottenheimer's departure. The decline prompted Hunt to hire a big name. Vermeil is a Super Bowl winner coming out of a one-year retirement. He's installing his own offensive system with Al Saunders expected as OC.

**Program pressure:** Moderate-High. Kansas City expects playoffs. Vermeil's pedigree raises expectations immediately. The roster has elite talent at key positions.

**Staff context:** Complete staff overhaul. Vermeil bringing his own people, blending St. Louis Rams staff members with new hires. Every position is open. Vermeil is an offensive coach who will be deeply involved in scheme and game-planning.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $35–50K
- Competition: Moderate
- System: Vermeil's pro-style offense (vertical passing, multi-personnel, TE-heavy concepts). Al Saunders expected as OC.
- Why it's open: Complete staff overhaul.
- Key personnel: Tony Gonzalez (TE — All-Pro, best TE in football). Priest Holmes emerging at RB. Trent Green at QB.

---

**Opening: Tight Ends Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $150–250K
- Competition: Moderate
- Why it's open: Full staff overhaul.
- Key personnel: Tony Gonzalez. The TE coach here coaches the best tight end in the NFL. Gonzalez is central to the offensive identity — 12 personnel, seam routes, matchup versatility. This is the highest-profile TE coaching job in the league.
- What the TE coach would do: Run the TE room with Gonzalez as the centerpiece. Technique refinement at the elite level. Route-running mastery. Blocking scheme integration. Game-plan installation for TE-heavy concepts.
- Notes: Vermeil values passion, teaching ability, and emotional connection with players. His coaching philosophy is relationship-driven.

### NEW YORK JETS

**Why jobs are open:** Al Groh resigned after one season (9-7) to become head coach at the University of Virginia. Herman Edwards hired from Tampa Bay Buccaneers (assistant HC under Tony Dungy).

**Organization context:** Third HC in three years (Parcells → Belichick one day → Groh → Edwards). Bill Parcells remains as GM/front-office power. The roster has talent (Curtis Martin at RB, Wayne Chrebet at WR, Vinny Testaverde aging at QB). The Jets made the AFC Championship in 1998 and have been declining since. Edwards is a first-time HC known for his motivational coaching style and defensive background (Dungy tree).

**Program pressure:** Moderate-High. New York market demands competitiveness. The Jets have talent but organizational instability has created dysfunction. Edwards needs to stabilize the culture.

**Staff context:** Full staff overhaul — third consecutive year of coaching turnover. Edwards bringing his own people from Tampa Bay. Some Parcells-era holdovers may remain. Offensive staff is a gap — Edwards's background is defensive.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $35–50K
- Competition: Moderate
- System: Pro-style (Parcells influence, evolving under Edwards). Edwards will hire an OC to determine specifics.
- Why it's open: Third consecutive full staff overhaul.

---

**Opening: Tight Ends Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $150–225K
- Competition: Moderate
- Key personnel: Anthony Becht (TE — second-year, needs development). Richie Anderson (FB/TE — versatile veteran).
- Notes: Edwards's Tampa Bay background (Dungy tree) values disciplined, technique-oriented coaching. Parcells as GM means his network may influence offensive hires.

### WASHINGTON REDSKINS

**Why jobs are open:** Norv Turner fired mid-season in 2000 (7-6 at the time). Terry Robiskie served as interim (finished 1-4). Marty Schottenheimer hired.

**Organization context:** Dan Snyder ownership — Year 2. Snyder is an aggressive, meddling owner who spends freely in free agency but creates organizational chaos. He fired Turner mid-season despite a winning record because he wanted a bigger name. The Redskins went 8-8 in 2000 (combined Turner/Robiskie). The roster has talent from Snyder's free-agent spending sprees (Bruce Smith, Deion Sanders — aging veterans on big contracts). Schottenheimer is a proven winner (200+ career victories) who demands organizational control.

**Program pressure:** High. Snyder expects immediate results. Schottenheimer has a strong track record but Snyder's interference will be constant. The power struggle between Schottenheimer (who wants personnel control) and Snyder (who wants to play GM) is inevitable.

**Staff context:** Complete staff overhaul. Schottenheimer bringing his own people. He fired Vinny Cerrato (Snyder's personnel guy) immediately — a power move that sets the tone. Every position is open.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $35–50K
- Competition: Moderate
- System: Schottenheimer's system — power-run, physical, ball-control offense.
- Why it's open: Complete staff overhaul.
- Caution: Schottenheimer is fired after one season (8-8) because Snyder wants to hire Steve Spurrier. Anyone joining this staff has a one-year shelf life.

---

**Opening: Tight Ends Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $150–250K
- Competition: High
- Key personnel: Stephen Alexander (TE — decent player). Schottenheimer's network is deep — experienced coaches are available.
- Notes: Schottenheimer fills his staff from a deep network built over 20+ years of NFL head coaching. Outside candidates face a significant network barrier.

## IN-SEASON CHANGES (2000)

### ARIZONA CARDINALS
- Vince Tobin fired mid-season (Week 7, 2-5 record). Dave McGinnis served as interim (4-7 the rest of the way, 6-10 overall). McGinnis was briefly announced as HC but the hire was rescinded amid controversy. Dave McGinnis was eventually re-hired formally in February 2001.
- Organization context: Bill Bidwill ownership. Perpetually dysfunctional franchise. The HC search was botched publicly. McGinnis is an underwhelming hire from within.
- Staff context: Partial overhaul. McGinnis retaining some staff, adding selectively. Limited openings compared to full-overhaul teams.

### CINCINNATI BENGALS
- Bruce Coslet resigned three games into the 2000 season (0-3). Dick LeBeau took over as interim HC (4-9 the rest of the way, 4-12 overall). LeBeau retained as full-time HC for 2001.
- Organization context: Mike Brown ownership — the most frugal owner in the NFL. Minimal resources. LeBeau is a defensive coaching legend but the roster is depleted. No staff overhaul expected — LeBeau keeping most of his people.
- Staff context: Minimal changes. LeBeau's continuity means few openings.
