## HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT

Each opening is a standalone block with standardized fields. Fields are consistent across all entries for RAG query reliability.

**Field definitions:**

- **Team:** NFL franchise name
- **Opening:** Role title
- **Level:** 0 = GA/QC, 1 = Position Coach
- **Salary Range:** Estimated 2001 compensation
- **Competition:** LOW, LOW-MODERATE, MODERATE, HIGH
- **HC:** Current head coach
- **HC Hired:** Date and origin
- **Staff Build Status:** FROM SCRATCH / PARTIAL OVERHAUL / CONTINUITY WITH CHANGES
- **System:** Offensive scheme being installed or retained
- **Why Open:** How and why this slot exists
- **Minimum Requirements (Realistic):** What the hiring staff actually expects
- **Decision Maker:** Who makes the hire for this specific role
- **Reports To:** Direct supervisor in this role
- **Hire Window:** When this role is likely filled
- **Key Personnel:** Relevant players or staff
- **Linked Dossier:** Path to the team's full dossier file

### Scoring Factors

- **Résumé weight:** Two years QC under Andy Reid on an 11-5 playoff team. This is a strong QC résumé line. Reid's name carries increasing market weight.
- **Playing pedigree:** HOF TE career. Instant player credibility. Matters more at TE coach openings than QC.
- **System match:** Stone knows Reid WCO only. Teams running WCO or WCO-adjacent schemes value this. Pro-style and power-run teams see partial transfer at best.
- **Networking (REC 48):** Stone has almost no coaching network outside Reid's staff. No connections to any HC on this list except a dormant Miami link to Butch Davis (score 55). Cold applications face a steep barrier.
- **Level 1 gaps:** No room ownership, no meeting command, no direct player-development reps. Level 1 readiness is "Emerging" — not "Ready." This is the single biggest drag on TE coach attraction scores.
- **Teaching (TCH 60):** Solid but unproven in a position-coach context. QC teaching support is not the same as running a room.
- **Media (MED 42):** Low. Matters in NY, Dallas. Irrelevant in Cleveland, KC, Buffalo.
