### INDIANAPOLIS COLTS — HC VACANCY

**Why open:** Jim Caldwell fired after 2-14 season. Peyton Manning missed entire 2011 season with neck surgery. The Colts hold the No. 1 overall pick (Andrew Luck).

**Organization context:** Jim Irsay ownership. The Colts are transitioning from the Peyton Manning era. Manning will be released — the franchise is rebuilding around Andrew Luck, the best QB prospect since John Elway. The entire offensive infrastructure needs to be reimagined for a new franchise QB.

**What the owner wants:** An HC who can develop Andrew Luck and build a modern offense around him. Defensive-minded HC with a strong OC is one model. Offensive-minded HC who calls his own plays is another.

**Roster context:** Luck (incoming No. 1 pick). Reggie Wayne (WR — veteran, elite). The roster is otherwise barren — 2-14 for a reason. Full rebuild needed.

**Candidates being discussed:** Bruce Arians, Chuck Pagano, Jeff Fisher, Jim Harbaugh (unavailable — already in SF). Offensive and defensive candidates both in play.

**HC Attraction Score: 6/10** — Luck is the ultimate draw. But Irsay may want a defensive HC paired with a strong OC rather than an offensive HC who controls the offense directly. Stone's offensive pedigree + college HC experience + QB development track record make him a real candidate, but the competition (Arians, Pagano, Fisher) is steep. Stone's lack of NFL coordinator title (he was PGC, not OC) may hurt against candidates with fuller NFL coordinator résumés.

**OC Attraction Score: 8/10** — If a defensive HC is hired, the OC job becomes the most important offensive hire in the NFL. Whoever designs the offense for Andrew Luck defines a franchise. Stone's dual-system mastery (Reid WCO + Turner Coryell), play-calling experience (college HC + NFL PGC), and QB development record make him an elite OC candidate for a Luck-development offense. The Coryell vertical concepts + WCO timing progressions = a modern passing attack built for a generational QB talent.

### ST. LOUIS RAMS — HC VACANCY

**Why open:** Steve Spagnuolo fired after three seasons (10-38). One of the worst three-year stretches in modern NFL history.

**Organization context:** Stan Kroenke ownership. Sam Bradford at QB (2010 No. 1 pick — talented but injury-prone). Steven Jackson at RB (veteran, elite). The roster has some pieces but needs wholesale rebuilding. The franchise is rumored to be considering relocation to Los Angeles (eventually happens in 2016).

**What the owner wants:** A veteran stabilizer who can bring credibility and structure to a franchise that has been a disaster. Kroenke values experience over potential.

**Candidates being discussed:** Jeff Fisher (the heavy favorite — veteran HC with 16 years in Tennessee). Gregg Williams. Various coordinator candidates.

**Roster context:** Bradford is the key. He needs development and protection. Steven Jackson provides a run-game foundation. No. 2 overall pick in 2012 draft (eventually traded for a haul — RG3 trade with Washington).

**HC Attraction Score: 4/10** — Fisher is the heavy favorite, and Kroenke wants a veteran HC. Stone's college HC experience is strong but Kroenke is looking for proven NFL HC credentials. The competition (Fisher) is overwhelming. Stone would need Fisher to decline for the door to open.

**OC Attraction Score: 6/10** — If Fisher is hired, he brings his own OC. If someone else gets the HC job, the OC opening is real. Bradford development needs passing-game expertise. Stone's WCO foundation could work with Bradford. But Fisher's Tennessee network is deep.

### JACKSONVILLE JAGUARS — HC VACANCY

**Why open:** Jack Del Rio fired mid-season after nine years (68-71). Mel Tucker served as interim (2-3). Franchise in decline.

**Organization context:** Shad Khan just purchased the team from Wayne Weaver (November 2011). Khan is a self-made billionaire — first minority owner in NFL history. He wants to build something lasting, not chase quick fixes. Blaine Gabbert at QB (2011 first-round pick — struggling badly, 5 TDs, 7 INTs as a starter). Maurice Jones-Drew at RB (elite). Marcedes Lewis at TE (physical, versatile, underutilized). Justin Blackmon drafted WR in 2012 (fifth overall). The roster has talent at skill positions but the offense has no identity.

**What the owner wants:** Khan wants a culture-builder who can install an offensive identity and develop Gabbert (or find his replacement). Khan values substance, intelligence, and long-term vision over big names.

**Candidates being discussed:** Mike Mularkey (Falcons OC — was an NFL TE himself), Dirk Koetter, various coordinator candidates. This search is wide open. Khan is new to the NFL and casting a broad net.

**Roster context:** Gabbert development is the critical question. MJD gives a run-game foundation. Marcedes Lewis is a weapon if properly used. Blackmon (2012 draft) adds a WR threat. The offense needs a schematic identity desperately.

**HC Attraction Score: 8/10** — This is Stone's best HC opportunity on the board.
- Khan wants a builder. Stone built Memphis from 4-8 to 9-4. That's exactly what Jacksonville needs.
- Mularkey (the likely competition) was an NFL TE — and so was Stone. If the criteria is "offensive mind who understands the TE position and can build an offensive identity," Stone's résumé is stronger than Mularkey's: HOF playing career vs. journeyman playing career, college HC experience vs. none, dual-system NFL coaching vs. single-tree.
- Marcedes Lewis at TE is the exact player Stone's TE-centric offensive philosophy is designed to maximize.
- Khan values intelligence and substance — Stone's PhD-level analytical mind, HOF career, and multi-tree coaching background fit Khan's personality.
- Gabbert development (or replacement) requires a coach who understands QB development. Stone developed QBs at Memphis and contributed to McNabb's development in Philly.
- Small-market patience. Jacksonville media is manageable (MED 66 is adequate).

**OC Attraction Score: 9/10** — If Stone doesn't get the HC job, the OC role under whoever is hired is equally attractive. Jacksonville needs an offensive identity. Stone can provide one. The TE-centric, dual-system attack Stone brings is exactly what this roster needs.

### TAMPA BAY BUCCANEERS — HC VACANCY

**Why open:** Raheem Morris fired after three seasons (17-31). Lost the locker room. Team collapsed from 10-6 (2010) to 4-12 (2011).

**Organization context:** Glazer family ownership. The Bucs have young talent: Josh Freeman at QB (talented but undisciplined, needs structure and development), LeGarrette Blount at RB, Mike Williams at WR. Doug Martin drafted in 2012 (first round). Vincent Jackson signed in 2012 FA. The roster has potential but needs a culture change and offensive direction.

**What the owner wants:** A disciplinarian who can install culture and accountability. The Glazers are leaning toward a hard-nosed, structure-first hire after Morris's lack of discipline. Greg Schiano (Rutgers HC) is the type they're looking at.

**Candidates being discussed:** Greg Schiano (Rutgers — program-builder, disciplinarian), various NFL coordinator candidates. The Glazers are specifically looking for a culture-change HC.

**Roster context:** Freeman needs development and discipline — a QB with all the physical talent but inconsistent decision-making. The TE position will gain relevance when Vincent Jackson arrives and the offense needs complementary pieces.

**HC Attraction Score: 5/10** — The Glazers are leaning toward a Schiano-type disciplinarian culture hire. Stone has the culture-building credentials (Memphis turnaround) but his coaching identity is more teacher/developer than disciplinarian. Stone's Disciplinarian trait is only 30 — the Glazers want someone higher on that axis. If Schiano doesn't work out, Stone could be a dark-horse candidate, but the philosophical mismatch with what the Glazers are specifically seeking works against him.

**OC Attraction Score: 6/10** — If a defensive HC is hired (Schiano), the OC job opens. Freeman needs a passing-game mind to develop him. Stone's teaching ability and QB development experience are relevant. But Schiano's rigid culture may clash with Stone's more player-development-oriented approach.

### MIAMI DOLPHINS — HC VACANCY

**Why open:** Tony Sparano fired after four seasons (29-32). Declined from 11-5 debut (2008) to 6-10 (2011). Stephen Ross ownership wants a big name.

**Organization context:** Stephen Ross ownership. Ross is aggressive and impatient — he publicly courted Jim Harbaugh while Sparano was still the HC. Jeff Ireland as GM (controversial, underperforming). Matt Moore at QB (placeholder). Ryan Tannehill will be drafted 8th overall in 2012. The franchise needs a QB-development HC.

**What the owner wants:** Ross wants a star hire. He pursued Harbaugh (unavailable). He wants someone with a brand, a vision, and a proven track record. Offensive-minded coaches preferred for QB development (Tannehill).

**Candidates being discussed:** Joe Philbin (Packers OC), Todd Bowles, various coordinator candidates. Ross wanted a big name but may settle for a respected coordinator.

**Roster context:** Tannehill (incoming 2012 draft) is the franchise pivot. Whoever coaches him defines the next era. The TE position is useful but not central. Brandon Marshall (WR) traded to Bears before 2012 — skill-position talent is thin.

**HC Attraction Score: 6/10** — Ross wants a bigger name than Stone, but Stone's combined résumé (HOF player + college HC + NFL PGC + dual-system mastery) is a substantive package. Tannehill development is the key selling point — Stone's QB development track record from Memphis and his passing-game expertise from San Diego are directly relevant. The competition (Philbin as the "safe Packers OC" pick) is moderate. Stone's MED 66 is adequate for the Miami market but Ross might want someone more polished.

**OC Attraction Score: 7/10** — If an HC is hired who doesn't call plays, the OC job is important. Tannehill development requires a skilled offensive mind. Stone's WCO background matches well with WCO-tree HCs. Mike Sherman connection (if Sherman is involved — he was Packers HC and appeared on Stone's early career opening lists) provides a warm network link.

### OAKLAND RAIDERS — HC VACANCY

**Why open:** Hue Jackson fired after one season (8-8). Al Davis died October 2011. Mark Davis taking over ownership. Reggie McKenzie hired as GM from Green Bay's front office. Complete organizational reset.

**Organization context:** Al Davis's death changed everything. The Raiders are transitioning from one-man autocratic rule to a modern front-office structure for the first time. McKenzie (from Green Bay's scouting department) is bringing a Packers-style organizational philosophy. Carson Palmer at QB (traded from Cincinnati mid-2011). Darren McFadden at RB (explosive, injury-prone). The roster has some talent but the infrastructure is being rebuilt from scratch.

**What the owner wants:** Mark Davis is figuring out ownership in real time. McKenzie wants a HC he can work with in a collaborative structure — the opposite of the Al Davis era. A coach who's comfortable sharing power with a GM, who won't demand total control.

**Candidates being discussed:** Dennis Allen (Broncos DC), various defensive coordinator candidates. McKenzie is looking for someone compatible with a modern front-office model.

**Roster context:** Palmer is a capable veteran QB. McFadden is explosive when healthy. But the organizational infrastructure is the real project, not the roster.

**HC Attraction Score: 4/10** — McKenzie is from the Green Bay front-office tree and may want a Green Bay/NFC-connected coach. Stone has no Raiders connection and no McKenzie connection. The organizational transition creates uncertainty about what the job even looks like. Allen (Broncos DC) is a safer, younger hire for McKenzie's collaborative model. Stone's age (44) and established identity may be viewed as less moldable than a younger candidate.

**OC Attraction Score: 5/10** — Palmer is a capable QB who doesn't need development — he needs a system. Stone could install one. But the organizational dysfunction and uncertainty about the HC hire make this a risky commitment.

### KANSAS CITY CHIEFS — HC VACANCY (SORT OF)

**Why open:** Todd Haley fired mid-season (Week 15, 2011). Romeo Crennel promoted from DC to interim HC (finished 2-1). Crennel retained as full-time HC for 2012 — but this is widely viewed as a placeholder hire, not a long-term solution. Scott Pioli as GM (underperforming, power struggles with ownership).

**Organization context:** Clark Hunt ownership. The Chiefs went 7-9 in 2011 despite starting 5-3. Haley's combative personality destroyed staff relationships. Crennel is a placeholder — he's a respected defensive coach but his previous HC tenure (Cleveland, 2005-2008, 24-40) was a failure. Matt Cassel at QB (not the answer). The roster has pieces (Jamaal Charles at RB — elite, Dwayne Bowe at WR — Pro Bowl) but no QB.

**The real opportunity is 2013.** Crennel goes 2-14 in 2012 and is fired. Andy Reid is hired as HC for 2013. If Stone waits one year, he could potentially reconnect with Reid in Kansas City — but that's speculative and depends on Reid wanting him back after 8+ years apart.

**HC Attraction Score: 2/10** — Crennel is already installed as HC for 2012. There is no open HC search. The 2013 cycle (when Crennel is fired) is the real opportunity, but that requires Stone to sit out 2012 or take a different job.

**OC Attraction Score: 4/10** — Crennel needs an OC. But joining a 2-14 staff for one year only to be swept out when the next HC arrives is a career waste. Not recommended unless Stone has no other options.
