# 1999 COACHING CAROUSEL — OPEN POSITIONS
## January 1999

## NFL OPENINGS

### BALTIMORE RAVENS

**Why jobs are open:** Ted Marchibroda fired after 1998 season. Brian Billick hired January 20, 1999 from Minnesota Vikings OC. Full staff overhaul in progress.

**Organization context:** Former Cleveland Browns franchise, relocated 1996. Ownership under Art Modell. Roster has pieces (Jonathan Ogden, Ray Lewis, Peter Boulware) but offense was bottom-third in the league. Billick hired specifically to fix the offense — he coordinated the 1998 Vikings record-setting 556-point season.

**Program pressure:** Moderate. Baltimore hasn't made playoffs yet as a franchise. Fans want competitiveness. Billick has time but expectations are rising.

**Staff context:** Billick bringing trusted people from Minnesota. Some positions locked. Others open to outside hires, especially offensive roles where he needs bodies who can teach his system.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $35–50K
- Tier: REALISTIC
- Competition: Moderate — Billick has Minnesota connections who may fill this first, but QC isn't a prestige role
- Why it fits Stone: Billick's offense is aggressive, vertical, multi-personnel — closest NFL system to the Coryell concepts Stone played in. TE-heavy personnel packages. Stone's playing knowledge of vertical passing and TE versatility is directly relevant to what Billick wants to build.
- Why it might not: Billick's inner circle from Minnesota is tight. Stone has no relationship with Billick. Breaking into an established coaching network as an outsider is hard at any level.
- What Stone would do: Break down opponent film, organize scout team, handle administrative tasks, learn Billick's offensive system from the ground up.

---

**Opening: Tight Ends Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $150–250K
- Tier: REACH
- Competition: High — position coach roles typically go to coaches with 3+ years experience
- Why it fits Stone: Hall of Fame TE teaching TEs. Billick values the TE position in his offensive scheme (12 personnel, TE versatility, seam routes). Stone's playing expertise is exactly what this role needs technically.
- Why it might not: Zero coaching experience. Billick likely wants someone who's run a position group meeting before. Stone has never designed a practice period, graded film for another player, or managed a group's schedule. Billick may not want to bet a position group on a first-timer, even one with Stone's résumé.
- What Stone would do: Coach TE technique (blocking, route running, releases), run position meetings, grade practice/game film, contribute to game-plan installation for TE-specific concepts.

### CAROLINA PANTHERS

**Why jobs are open:** Dom Capers fired after 1998 season (4-12). George Seifert hired. Full staff overhaul.

**Organization context:** Expansion team (1995). Made NFC Championship in year 2 under Capers, then collapsed. Seifert is a two-time Super Bowl winner (49ers 1989, 1994). Ownership under Jerry Richardson — demands professionalism and results.

**Program pressure:** High. Richardson is impatient. Seifert expected to turn the franchise around quickly. Roster has some talent (Kevin Greene departing, but Wesley Walls at TE, Muhsin Muhammad at WR).

**Staff context:** Seifert bringing his own people, heavily drawn from the 49ers coaching tree. Very established network. Outside hires will fill gaps his network can't cover.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $30–45K
- Tier: REALISTIC
- Competition: Moderate — Seifert's 49ers network is deep but QC roles are low priority for established coaches
- Why it fits Stone: Pro-style offense. Seifert values football intelligence and preparation. Wesley Walls is the starting TE — Stone could add value by understanding the position at an elite level even in a QC role.
- Why it might not: Seifert is old-school. May view Stone's PhD and academic background skeptically. Prefers coaches who came up through the grind, not former stars.
- What Stone would do: Standard QC duties — film breakdown, scout team, administrative support. Would learn Seifert's pro-style system.

---

**Opening: Wide Receivers Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $175–275K
- Tier: REACH
- Competition: High — Seifert's 49ers tree produces WR coaches. Muhsin Muhammad is a rising star who needs good coaching.
- Why it fits Stone: Route craft knowledge (90 rating) translates across TE/WR. Stone understands releases, stems, spacing at an elite level.
- Why it might not: Stone played TE, not WR. Different position nuances (press releases, deep speed routes, return duties). Zero coaching experience. Seifert likely fills this from his network.
- What Stone would do: Coach WR technique, run position meetings, handle WR film study and game-plan installation.

### CHICAGO BEARS

**Why jobs are open:** Dave Wannstedt fired after 1998 season (4-12). Dick Jauron hired. Full staff overhaul.

**Organization context:** Historic franchise in disarray. Bad roster. Jauron is a defensive-minded HC (former DB coach under Bill Parcells). Offense needs wholesale rebuilding. Soldier Field, big market, high media scrutiny.

**Program pressure:** Moderate-high. Bears haven't been relevant since 1985. Fans are restless. Jauron gets some patience but not much. Media market is demanding.

**Staff context:** Jauron building a defensive staff from his own network. Offensive staff is wide open — he needs an OC and full offensive assistant group. Less competition for offensive roles because Jauron doesn't have deep offensive connections.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $30–45K
- Tier: REALISTIC
- Competition: Low-moderate — Jauron's network is defensive. Offensive QC is a gap he needs to fill, likely from outside his circle.
- Why it fits Stone: Offensive QC on a team that desperately needs offensive help. Jauron will hire an OC who sets the system — Stone would learn whatever that system is from the ground floor. Low competition for the role.
- Why it might not: Jauron doesn't know Stone. No relationship, no connection. Chicago is a tough media market for a first-timer to learn in.
- What Stone would do: Support the new OC with film breakdown, scout team management, administrative tasks.

---

**Opening: Tight Ends Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $125–200K
- Tier: REACH
- Competition: Moderate — TE coach is a lower-profile hire, less competition than WR or OL coach
- Why it fits Stone: TE position. Stone's expertise. Bears' TE group needs development.
- Why it might not: Jauron has no reason to take a chance on a first-time coach when experienced candidates are available. No personal connection.
- What Stone would do: Run the TE room — meetings, practice plans, technique work, film study, game-plan installation for TE assignments.

### CLEVELAND BROWNS (EXPANSION)

**Why jobs are open:** Brand new franchise. Reactivated after three-year suspension. Every single position is open. Chris Palmer hired as HC. Building entire organization from scratch.

**Organization context:** Expansion team with expansion roster. Front office run by Dwight Clark (former 49er) and Carmen Policy. Palmer is an offensive coach (former Jaguars OC). The 1999 Browns will be one of the worst teams in football — everyone involved knows this. Expansion draft and regular draft will stock the roster with castoffs and rookies.

**Program pressure:** Low short-term, high long-term. Cleveland is a rabid football city. Patience exists for year 1. By year 2, wins are expected. Palmer went 5-27 and was fired after two seasons.

**Staff context:** Building from zero. Palmer needs coaches at every level. Less able to be picky. Willing to take chances on non-traditional hires because traditional candidates don't want to join an expansion team.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $30–45K
- Tier: REALISTIC (Strong)
- Competition: Low — expansion teams struggle to attract established coaches. QC roles on a team projected to win 3 games aren't desirable.
- Why it fits Stone: Easiest door to walk through. Palmer needs people. Stone's playing credibility adds legitimacy to a staff that needs it. Palmer runs West Coast concepts — Stone would learn a new system. Low competition means Stone's playing résumé carries more weight.
- Why it might not: The Browns will be terrible. Losing culture from day one. The organization is chaotic — new everything, no infrastructure, no established processes. Learning to coach in this environment is trial by fire in the worst way.
- What Stone would do: Standard QC. But on an expansion team, QC coaches end up doing everything — scouting, recruiting, film, logistics, whatever needs doing.

---

**Opening: Tight Ends Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $100–175K
- Tier: REALISTIC-REACH (Better odds than elsewhere)
- Competition: Low-moderate — expansion teams take chances. Palmer might value a Hall of Fame TE to add credibility.
- Why it fits Stone: Expansion teams are more willing to hire non-traditional candidates. Stone's name recognition helps a franchise that needs to establish legitimacy with players and fans. TE position is Stone's expertise.
- Why it might not: Even expansion teams prefer some coaching experience. Palmer may want a known quantity to help install his West Coast system. Stone doesn't know the West Coast offense.
- What Stone would do: Build a TE room from scratch with expansion draft players and rookies. Maximum teaching opportunity — but also maximum difficulty because the talent will be limited.

### GREEN BAY PACKERS

**Why jobs are open:** Mike Holmgren resigned to become HC/GM in Seattle. Ray Rhodes hired. Staff transition in progress.

**Organization context:** Marquee franchise. Brett Favre at QB. Playoff roster. Rhodes inherits a good team but must put his stamp on it. Rhodes was fired by Philadelphia after 1998 — this is his second chance.

**Program pressure:** Very high. Packers were a Super Bowl contender under Holmgren. Anything less than playoffs is failure. Rhodes is expected to maintain, not rebuild.

**Staff context:** Some Holmgren holdovers staying. Rhodes bringing some of his Philadelphia staff. Mix of continuity and change. Premium organization — competition for staff roles is high.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $35–50K
- Tier: REALISTIC (but competitive)
- Competition: High — Green Bay is a desirable organization. Even QC roles attract quality candidates.
- Why it fits Stone: Elite organization. Learning environment. Brett Favre. Playoff-caliber team means Stone sees winning football from day one.
- Why it might not: High competition. Rhodes doesn't know Stone. Packers have their pick of candidates.
- What Stone would do: Standard QC in a winning environment. Better learning experience than an expansion team, but harder to stand out.

### KANSAS CITY CHIEFS

**Why jobs are open:** Marty Schottenheimer resigned after 1998 season. Gunther Cunningham promoted internally from DC. Some staff continuity, but changes at coordinator level and select position coach roles.

**Organization context:** Solid franchise under Lamar Hunt ownership. Good roster (Derrick Thomas, Tony Gonzalez at TE). Cunningham is a defensive coach — offensive staff has more flux.

**Program pressure:** Moderate. Chiefs are perennial playoff contenders. Cunningham expected to maintain that. Tony Gonzalez is emerging as the best TE in football.

**Staff context:** More continuity than a full HC change. Some Schottenheimer assistants staying. Fewer openings overall, but offensive staff gaps exist.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $30–45K
- Tier: REALISTIC (limited openings)
- Competition: Moderate — fewer openings means fewer slots, but less turnover also means less competition per slot.
- Why it fits Stone: Tony Gonzalez. The best young TE in football is in Kansas City. Stone's TE expertise would be immediately relevant, even in a QC role. Stone could learn by watching Gonzalez — and eventually could add value coaching alongside him.
- Why it might not: Cunningham is a DC. Offensive staff is someone else's domain. Stone has no connection to Cunningham or the KC organization. Fewer openings overall.
- What Stone would do: Offensive film breakdown, scout team, supporting the TE/offensive staff. Proximity to Gonzalez is the real draw.

### PHILADELPHIA EAGLES

**Why jobs are open:** Ray Rhodes fired after 1998 season (3-13). Andy Reid hired from Green Bay Packers (QB coach). Full staff overhaul.

**Organization context:** Franchise in bad shape. Bad roster, bad record, low morale. Reid is 40, a first-time HC, and largely unknown nationally. He's installing the West Coast offense. Owner Jeffrey Lurie is patient but expects a plan.

**Program pressure:** Low initially. Reid gets time because expectations are rock bottom. But Philly media is brutal — patience has limits.

**Staff context:** Reid building from scratch. Bringing some Green Bay connections (Jim Johnson as DC). Offensive staff is Reid's domain — he's an offensive coach who will be deeply involved in scheme. Needs assistants who can teach his system.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $30–45K
- Tier: REALISTIC
- Competition: Low-moderate — Reid is unknown, Philly is a losing franchise, QC roles here aren't glamorous.
- Why it fits Stone: Reid values teaching and player development. Stone's teaching instincts align with Reid's philosophy. Low-pressure environment to learn. Reid is building something — Stone would be in from the ground floor.
- Why it might not: Reid is installing West Coast offense — a system Stone has no experience with. Reid may want people who already know WCO terminology and concepts.
- What Stone would do: Learn the West Coast offense from the inside. Film breakdown, scout team, administrative support.
- Hidden upside: Reid becomes one of the greatest coaches in NFL history. Getting in early with Reid's coaching tree is a career-defining move — but nobody knows that in January 1999.

---

**Opening: Tight Ends Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $100–175K
- Tier: REACH
- Competition: Moderate — Reid's staff isn't fully formed. He's less established, may be more open to unconventional hires.
- Why it fits Stone: TE position. Reid's West Coast offense uses TEs extensively. Chad Lewis is the starting TE — decent player who needs development.
- Why it might not: Reid may want someone who already knows WCO TE assignments. Stone doesn't know the system.
- What Stone would do: Coach TE technique within Reid's WCO framework. Would need to learn the system while teaching it.

### SAN DIEGO CHARGERS

**Why jobs are open:** Kevin Gilbride fired during 1998 season. June Jones finished as interim. Mike Riley hired from Oregon State. Full staff overhaul.

**Organization context:** Stone's former team. He played here 12 years (1987-1998). Franchise is in bad shape — bad roster, low attendance, stadium issues. Riley is a college coach making the NFL jump. Ownership under Alex Spanos is budget-conscious.

**Program pressure:** Low. Expectations are minimal. Riley is a development hire. Spanos just wants competence and cost control.

**Staff context:** Riley coming from college. Bringing some Oregon State assistants but needs NFL-experienced coaches to fill gaps. The staff will be a mix of college guys and NFL veterans willing to take a chance on a rebuilding team.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $25–40K (budget-conscious org)
- Tier: REALISTIC (Strong)
- Competition: Low — rebuilding team with a college coach, QC roles aren't attracting top candidates.
- Why it fits Stone: He knows the building. He knows the city. He knows people in the organization. Shortest learning curve of any opening. Riley might value having a franchise legend on staff for credibility with players and fans.
- Why it might not: Riley may want to build his own culture without legacy players looking over his shoulder. Stone's stature could be intimidating to a college coach making his first NFL hire.
- What Stone would do: Bridge between Riley's college system and NFL pace. Film breakdown, scout team, organizational knowledge.

---

**Opening: Tight Ends Coach**
- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $100–150K (lower end, budget org)
- Tier: REALISTIC-REACH
- Competition: Low — San Diego isn't a destination job. Riley's candidate pool is thin for NFL position coaches.
- Why it fits Stone: Former team. TE expertise. Riley needs NFL credibility. Stone is a franchise icon who would be the most famous person on the coaching staff.
- Why it might not: Riley may feel threatened by having a Hall of Famer on staff who has more football credibility than he does. Power dynamic is awkward.
- What Stone would do: Run the TE room on a rebuilding team. Development-focused. Low win pressure, high teaching opportunity.

### SEATTLE SEAHAWKS

**Why jobs are open:** Dennis Erickson fired after 1998 season (8-8). Mike Holmgren hired from Green Bay as HC and GM. Full staff overhaul.

**Organization context:** Paul Allen ownership (Microsoft money). Holmgren given full control — HC and GM. Installing West Coast offense. Building toward a contender. Good resources, patient ownership.

**Program pressure:** Moderate. Allen is patient and wealthy. Holmgren gets time and money. But Holmgren is also expected to produce — he won a Super Bowl in Green Bay.

**Staff context:** Holmgren bringing a significant portion of his Green Bay staff. Very tight inner circle. Outside hires fill the gaps his network can't cover. Premium coaching destination because of Holmgren's reputation and Allen's resources.

---

**Opening: Offensive Quality Control**
- Level: 0 (GA/QC)
- Salary: $40–55K (Allen's money = better pay even at QC level)
- Tier: REALISTIC (but very competitive)
- Competition: High — Holmgren's name attracts quality candidates at every level. His GB staff wants to follow him.
- Why it fits Stone: Master class in the West Coast offense. Holmgren is one of the best offensive minds in football. Learning under him would accelerate Stone's development. Well-resourced organization.
- Why it might not: Holmgren's inner circle is locked. He's bringing his people. QC roles may already be filled by GB assistants following him. Stone has no connection to Holmgren.
- What Stone would do: WCO boot camp. Film breakdown, scout team, absorb everything Holmgren teaches.

## COLLEGE OPENINGS

### UNIVERSITY OF MIAMI — GRADUATE ASSISTANT (OFFENSE)

- Level: 0 (GA)
- Salary: $15–25K (plus tuition waiver if applicable)
- Tier: REALISTIC
- Why it's open: Normal GA turnover. Butch Davis is HC (since 1995). Staff otherwise stable.
- Organization context: Stone's alma mater. Miami is a top-15 program rebuilding toward national contention. Davis runs a pro-style offense. Strong recruiting culture — South Florida is a talent pipeline.
- Program pressure: Moderate. Miami is rising under Davis but not yet back to the 1980s dynasty level. Davis has time.
- Competition: Low — GA roles aren't glamorous. But Stone would be overqualified, which could be awkward.
- Why it fits Stone: Alma mater. Connections. Recruiting value — a Hall of Fame Miami alum helps land South Florida recruits. Pro-style offense is familiar. Davis would likely welcome Stone.
- Why it might not: GA pay is poverty-level. Stone doesn't need the money but the role is menial — copying playbooks, running errands, breaking down film at 2 AM. Status drop from Hall of Fame player to GA is steep. College coaching is a different world — recruiting, NCAA compliance, 18-year-old players.
- What Stone would do: Learn college coaching from the ground up. Film breakdown, scout team, recruiting trips, administrative work. Would get exposure to recruiting, which is a blind spot.

### MID-MAJOR FBS — TIGHT ENDS COACH (VARIOUS)

- Level: 1 (Position Coach)
- Salary: $40–80K
- Tier: REALISTIC-REACH
- Why they're open: Normal staff turnover at smaller programs. New HC hires at Group of 5 schools create cascading openings.
- Organization context: Smaller programs (Conference USA, MAC, WAC, Sun Belt in 1999) have fewer resources but less competition for staff roles. Stone's name recognition would be a significant draw.
- Program pressure: Varies. Some rebuilding, some stable. Generally lower pressure than NFL or Power conference.
- Competition: Low — mid-major TE coach roles don't attract deep candidate pools.
- Why it fits Stone: Lower barrier to entry. Programs would value the credibility of a Hall of Fame player. TE coaching is Stone's specialty. Recruiting boost is real — high school players want to be coached by a Pro Bowler.
- Why it might not: Lifestyle downgrade. Facilities, pay, and resources are a fraction of NFL. Stone would need to learn NCAA recruiting rules, compliance, academic eligibility — none of which he knows. College TEs are not NFL TEs — different skill level, different teaching approach.
- What Stone would do: Run a TE room at the college level. Teach fundamentals to less experienced players. Recruit. Learn college coaching culture.

---

### POWER CONFERENCE — OFFENSIVE QUALITY CONTROL (VARIOUS)

- Level: 0 (QC)
- Salary: $25–40K
- Tier: REALISTIC
- Why they're open: Staff turnover from the 1998-99 coaching carousel. SEC, Big 12, Pac-10, Big Ten, ACC programs with new coaches are building staffs.
- Organization context: Better resources than mid-majors. Higher-level football. But college QC roles are grind positions — long hours, minimal recognition.
- Competition: Low-moderate — college QC roles attract graduate assistants and young coaches, not Hall of Fame players.
- Why it fits Stone: Power conference programs play high-level football. Better learning environment than mid-major. Stone's name helps recruiting even from a QC role.
- Why it might not: Same college coaching unknowns — recruiting, compliance, younger players. QC at a college program is arguably less valuable for NFL coaching development than QC at an NFL team.
- What Stone would do: Film breakdown, scout team, recruiting support. Learn college football operations.
