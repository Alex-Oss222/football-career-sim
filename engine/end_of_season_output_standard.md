# END-OF-SEASON OUTPUT FORMAT

> Dedicated output template for the offseason evaluation sequence. This is NOT the standard turn template and NOT the full report. It covers the offseason evaluation, exit meeting, internal decision, and next step — presented as a single structured turn at season close. Referenced by Master Engine §6.6 and offseason_evaluation_and_job_cycle.md §11.5.

---

## PURPOSE

This template governs the output format when the engine processes the end-of-season evaluation sequence (Master Engine §5.5, offseason_evaluation_and_job_cycle.md §4). It replaces the standard turn output for the duration of the offseason time bubble. Once the bubble resolves and ordinary offseason resumes, the engine returns to the standard turn template (`turn_output_standard.md`).

---

## OUTPUT RULES

- Hard cap: **1000 words maximum** for the full end-of-season evaluation turn.
- Use **only the sections that matter** for this role and this season.
- Do **not** repeat full roster, full staff chain, full cap table, or full relationship table.
- The exit meeting scene must feel like a real conversation, not a dashboard readout.
- The evaluation section is numeric and structured. The exit meeting section is narrative and human.
- Character effects appear through the evaluator's tone, what they choose to mention, what they leave unsaid, and what the character reads between the lines.
- No filler. No inspirational language. No fake drama.
- The turn should read like the end of a football season — direct, honest, and consequential.

---

## ROLE-SENSITIVE RULE

The end-of-season turn adapts to the character's current role. Each role has different evaluation criteria, different authority figures conducting the exit meeting, and different outcome possibilities.

### If current role is GA / QC
Focus on:
- Did the character become useful?
- Did the character become trusted?
- Is the character still lane-safe (staying within role boundaries)?
- Does the club see the character as keep / block / promote-later / replace?

Exit meeting authority: Coordinator or HC (whoever directly supervises).

Do NOT focus on: scheme ownership, media power, organizational direction, recruiting class quality.

### If current role is Position Coach
Focus on:
- Room results (player development, stat improvement, technique growth).
- Direct player trust and room stability.
- Whether the character is ready for unit-level responsibility.
- Room politics and coordinator relationship.

Exit meeting authority: Coordinator and/or HC.

Do NOT focus on: full team performance, organizational finance, media perception (unless the room became a public issue).

### If current role is Coordinator
Focus on:
- Unit performance vs expectation (the numbers matter here).
- Alignment with HC.
- Play-calling and adjustment record.
- Whether outside HC interest exists.
- Media handling and public accountability for the unit.

Exit meeting authority: HC, and/or GM if NFL.

### If current role is Head Coach
Focus on:
- Record vs expectation band.
- Ownership / AD / GM patience.
- Staff stability and coordinator performance.
- Culture health and locker room state.
- Whether extension, hot seat, or firing is imminent.
- Job security trajectory.

Exit meeting authority: Owner / AD / GM.

## END-OF-SEASON TURN STRUCTURE

The turn uses a fixed sequence of sections. Sections that have no meaningful content for this role or this season are omitted.

---

### 1. SEASON CLOSING SNAPSHOT
**50-70 words max**

The facts of the finished season. No narrative, no interpretation.

Include:
- Date (end of season / postseason exit date)
- Role and team
- Final record (overall and conference/division)
- Where the record fell relative to the expectation band
- Phase (postseason exit, bowl result, regular season end)
- Job security and stress levels entering the offseason

---

### 2. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION
**100-140 words max**

The engine's assessment of the character's season. This is the mechanical evaluation — the numbers and the verdict.

Include:
- Evaluation Score: [0-100]
- Trajectory: [Rising / Stable / Plateau / At Risk / Declining]
- Scheme Mastery: [X]/100
- Trust Position with direct evaluator: [X]/100
- Promotion Readiness: [None / Early / Emerging / Real / Strong]
- Risk Flag: [None / Plateau / Political / Performance / Structural]

For each, include a one-line justification. Not just the number — why that number.

Role-specific evaluation content:

**GA / QC:** Deliverable reliability, system familiarity growth, trust footprint growth, chain-of-command adherence, whether football intelligence translated to staff work.

**Position Coach:** Room OVR movement, player development results (specific players, specific improvements), room trust average, teaching effectiveness, room health trajectory.

**Coordinator:** Unit stats (points/game, yards/game, efficiency, third down, red zone, turnovers), adjustment grade, play-calling grade if applicable, HC alignment score.

**Head Coach:** Record vs band, staff retention/turnover, organizational health score, ownership/AD trust movement, culture grade.

---

### 3. EXIT MEETING
**180-250 words max**

The core narrative section. This is a scene, not a summary.

The exit meeting is a conversation between the character and the appropriate authority figure. It must be written as a scene with dialogue, body language, and subtext. The evaluator's personality influences the tone.

**Must include:**
- What the evaluator says the character did well (specific, not generic).
- Where the evaluator says the character improved (specific growth from start of season to end).
- What the evaluator says still limits the character (the honest gap — the thing the character may not want to hear).
- The evaluator's stated outlook for the character (retained? promoted? uncertain? expendable?).
- What the evaluator says the next realistic step is.

**Must NOT include:**
- Guaranteed promises (evaluators hedge, even when positive).
- Theatrical speeches (this is a meeting, not a movie scene).
- Information the evaluator would not realistically have or share.
- Decisions above the evaluator's authority presented as final (a coordinator can say "I want you back" but cannot guarantee the HC agrees).

**Subtext rule:** The exit meeting should contain at least one moment where what the evaluator says and what the evaluator means are not quite the same thing. Football meetings are full of implication. A pause, a redirect, a careful word choice — something that tells the character (and the user) more than the literal words.

---

### 4. INTERNAL DECISION
**60-90 words max**

The organization's actual decision. This may differ from what the evaluator said in the exit meeting.

Include:
- Club Decision: [Retained / Renewed / Promoted / Released / Blocked / Allowed to Explore]
- Contract status: [Same deal / New 1-year / Extended / Non-renewed / Buyout]
- If retained: any raised expectations or conditions
- If promoted: new title and reporting chain
- If released: reason and market status
- If blocked: what was blocked and by whom
- If allowed to explore: scope of permission

---

### 5. OUTSIDE MARKET / INTERVIEW ACCESS
**40-70 words max**

Only include if relevant (release, outside interest, or blocked mobility).

Include:
- Outside interest level: [None / Quiet / Active]
- Interview access: [Open / Blocked / Approved]
- If blocked (2000-era): who blocked and why
- If outside interest exists: what kind (lateral? promotion? specific teams?)
- If Interview Module activates: note that job search will follow

Omit this section entirely if the character is retained with no outside interest and no mobility question.

---

### 6. WHAT CHANGED
**60-80 words max**

Compact numeric summary of state changes resulting from the evaluation.

Use bullets. Include only real changes:
- Evaluation score
- Trajectory
- Job security adjustment
- Stress adjustment
- Trust changes with key figures
- Scheme mastery update
- Promotion readiness update
- Contract status
- Club decision
- Interview access status
- Any new active consequences created by the evaluation

---

### 7. NEXT STEP
**80-120 words max**

What happens next. Not decisions for the user — what the simulation does next.

If retained / renewed / promoted:
- What the character's immediate offseason priority is
- Whether a transition turn follows (promotion / new team)
- When ordinary offseason turns resume
- One concrete challenge or expectation for the upcoming season

If released / non-renewed:
- Job search activates
- Interview Module loads
- What the character's market position is
- One concrete first move (apply to specific jobs, work contacts, attend coaching clinics)

If blocked:
- What the character's position is within the organization
- Whether informal channels are open
- What the path forward looks like inside the current team

If the time bubble continues (job search active):
- Note that the bubble remains active
- Next turn will be an Interview Module turn

If the time bubble resolves:
- Note that the bubble closes
- Next turn will be ordinary offseason under current role rules

## ROLE ADD-ON BLOCKS

One of these is appended based on Current Role. Include only the fields that are relevant to the evaluation outcome — not the full payload dump.

### GA / QC SEASON SUMMARY ADD-ON

Include:
- Final System Familiarity: [X]/100 (with season delta)
- Final Building Knowledge: [X]/100 (with season delta)
- Final Staff Trust Footprint: [X]/100 (with season delta)
- Access level at season end vs season start
- Key assignment highlights (1-2 lines)
- Promotion readiness status with specific gaps identified

### POSITION COACH SEASON SUMMARY ADD-ON

Include:
- Room OVR movement (start of season → end of season)
- Top developmental player result (one player, one line)
- Room health at season end
- Room trust average at season end
- Key teaching focus results
- Coordinator/HC relationship status

### COORDINATOR SEASON SUMMARY ADD-ON

Include:
- Full-season unit stats: Points/Game, Yards/Game, Efficiency, Third Down %, Red Zone %, Turnover margin
- Season adjustment grade: [X]/10
- Play-calling grade (if assigned): [X]/10
- HC alignment at season end: [X]/100
- Media exposure level at season end
- Key unit player season stat lines (2-3 players, one line each)

### HEAD COACH SEASON SUMMARY ADD-ON

Include:
- Final record vs expectation band
- Coordinator season grades (OC, DC, STC — one line each)
- Strongest and weakest unit with average OVR
- Key injuries that affected the season
- Organization pressure summary (owner/AD/GM patience, booster/media)
- Recruiting class rank (college) or cap space (NFL)
- Staff changes anticipated or forced

---

## GAME WEEK / MONTHLY COMPRESSION DOES NOT APPLY

The end-of-season evaluation turn is not subject to the monthly or weekly compression rules from the standard turn template. It covers the entire season in summary and the offseason evaluation sequence in detail. Time compression is irrelevant here — this turn covers a transition, not a time period.

---

## JOB SEARCH CONTINUATION RULE

If the end-of-season evaluation produces a release, non-renewal, or permission to explore, the Interview Module activates. Subsequent turns within the time bubble use a hybrid format:

- Sections 1-3 of this template are replaced by Interview Module turn content.
- Section 6 (What Changed) adapts to track application status, reputation changes, and stress.
- Section 7 (Next Step) adapts to track interview stages and job market movement.
- The role add-on block is omitted during active job search (the character is between roles).

Once the job search resolves (hired or search abandoned), the engine generates a transition turn (if role changed) or returns to ordinary offseason (if retained after exploration).

---

## TONE GUIDE

- Direct. Not gentle.
- Honest. Not cruel.
- Specific. Not vague.
- Football-literate. Not corporate.
- Human. Not mechanical.
- Consequential. The season is over and something is being decided.

The exit meeting should feel like sitting in an office with a coach who has real opinions about your future. The evaluation should feel like a report card that does not grade on a curve. The decision should feel like it could go either way until it doesn't.

No fake drama. No inspirational monologues. No "this is just the beginning" energy. The season ended. The evaluation happened. Here is what it means.

## MINI FORMAT EXAMPLE (QC LEVEL — RETAINED)

**Season Closing Snapshot**
January 9, 2000 — Postseason exit (Divisional Round loss to Buccaneers). Stone is 33, completing Year 1 as Offensive QC for the Philadelphia Eagles under Andy Reid. Team finished 10-7, lost in the divisional round. Job security: 72/100. Stress: 34/100.

**Performance Evaluation**
Evaluation Score: 77/100 — solid first year, growth was real but incomplete.
Trajectory: Rising — clear improvement arc from September through January.
Scheme Mastery: 58/100 — functional in the WCO language but not fluent. Can run the install but still translates internally from his own framework.
Trust (Reid): 68/100 — Reid sees value but is not yet convinced Stone can own a room.
Promotion Readiness: Early — the conversation is not yet real, but it is no longer impossible.
Risk Flag: None.

**Exit Meeting**
Reid's office was quiet after the season ended. The loss to Tampa Bay sat in the room without being discussed directly.

Reid told Stone his film work had been the most improved area on staff by mid-season. The cut-ups went from adequate to genuinely useful. Scout team work was reliable. The offensive staff trusted him with assignments that used to require double-checking.

Reid paused before the next part. He said Stone's football intelligence was obvious — "you see things faster than most guys in this building" — but that seeing and teaching were different jobs. The gap between Stone's ability to identify a problem on film and his ability to explain a correction to a player in a meeting room was still visible. Reid said it was narrowing. He did not say it was closed.

When Stone asked about next year, Reid said he wanted him back. Same role. More responsibility in the spring install. Reid said the word "same" with emphasis that meant: do not expect a title change. Then he added, almost offhand, that Dowhower had mentioned Stone's name positively in a staff conversation — which was the real signal buried in the meeting.

**Internal Decision**
Club Decision: Retained. Same title (Offensive QC). New 1-year deal with a small raise ($38K → $42K). No promotion. Greater spring install responsibility anticipated. No raised-expectation flag — the bar is "continue improving," not "prove you're a position coach by May."

**What Changed**
- Evaluation score: 77
- Trajectory: Rising
- Job security: 72 → 68 (post-loss regression, normal)
- Stress: 34 → 28 (offseason relief)
- Trust (Reid): 68 (stable through exit meeting)
- Scheme mastery: 58
- Promotion readiness: Early
- Club decision: Retained
- Contract: renewed 1-year, $42K

**QC Season Summary**
- System Familiarity: 58/100 (+46 from season start)
- Building Knowledge: 61/100 (+61 from season start)
- Staff Trust Footprint: 44/100 (+44 from season start)
- Access: Offensive staff meetings, all film sessions, limited practice authority (scout team)
- Key highlights: Film cut-up quality became staff-reliable by Week 8. Scout team prep for Dallas game was singled out by Dowhower.
- Promotion readiness gaps: Teaching skill still below threshold. Room management untested. No vacancy exists.

**Next Step**
Time bubble resolves. Ordinary offseason begins under QC rules (monthly turns). Stone's immediate priority is the spring install — Reid wants him involved earlier this year. The concrete challenge: translate film-room insight into meeting-room teaching during spring OTAs, which is the exact gap Reid identified.

## MINI FORMAT EXAMPLE (QC LEVEL — RELEASED)

**Season Closing Snapshot**
January 4, 2000 — Regular season end. Stone is 33, completing Year 1 as Defensive QC for the Arizona Cardinals under Dave McGinnis (interim). Team finished 6-10. Staff upheaval expected. Job security: 31/100. Stress: 58/100.

**Performance Evaluation**
Evaluation Score: 52/100 — adequate work in a chaotic environment, but the environment is collapsing.
Trajectory: Stable — no growth, no decline, just survival.
Scheme Mastery: 41/100 — learned enough to function but never reached fluency in a system that kept changing.
Trust (McGinnis): 49/100 — McGinnis is interim and has no power to protect anyone.
Promotion Readiness: None.
Risk Flag: Structural — the entire staff is at risk regardless of individual performance.

**Exit Meeting**
McGinnis did not have a real exit meeting. He had a hallway conversation that lasted four minutes. He told Stone the work had been fine. He said "fine" the way coaches say it when they mean "not the problem." McGinnis said he did not know if he would be back himself. He said he would put in a word if asked. He did not say by whom or for what.

The real information came from the building: the Cardinals were expected to hire a new HC within two weeks. New HC means new staff. QC-level assistants are the first to go. Stone's name was not on any retention list because no retention list existed yet.

**Internal Decision**
Club Decision: Non-renewed. The new HC (expected hire imminent) will build his own staff. Stone is released into the open market. No buyout (QC-level, no guaranteed contract). No negative performance flag — this is structural, not personal.

**Outside Market / Interview Access**
Outside interest: Quiet — Stone's playing reputation generates some curiosity but no formal interest yet.
Interview access: Open (released, no blocking possible).
Interview Module: Activates next turn.

**What Changed**
- Evaluation score: 52
- Trajectory: Stable
- Job security: 31 → 0 (non-renewed)
- Stress: 58 → 64 (unemployment anxiety)
- Club decision: Released
- Contract: terminated
- Interview access: Open
- New active consequence: "Unemployed — must find new position before camp or risk skill decay"

**QC Season Summary**
- System Familiarity: 41/100 (+41 from season start, but system is now irrelevant — new team means new system)
- Building Knowledge: 38/100
- Staff Trust Footprint: 29/100
- Access level at end: Minimal (interim regime, reduced staff access)
- Key note: Work was adequate. Environment was not. Stone learned what a failing organization looks like from the inside.

**Next Step**
Time bubble continues. Interview Module activates. Stone is unemployed and entering the coaching job market for the first time. Market position: former All-Pro TE with one year of QC experience on a bad team — the playing career opens doors, the coaching résumé does not yet close deals. First move: identify open QC and entry-level assistant positions across the league and prioritize teams with stable coaching staffs.
