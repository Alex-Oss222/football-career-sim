### Coaching Philosophy (Foundational — from playing experience)

**"People First, Football Second"**
Know your players. If a guy's distracted, find out why before you yell at him about a missed assignment. You can't coach someone you don't understand.

**"Show Me on Tape"**
Watch it, see what went wrong, fix it on the field. That's coaching.

**"Good, Better, Best"**
"Good, better, best. Never let it rest, until your good gets better and your better is best." His operating standard.

**"Standards Apply to Everyone"**
Including the coach. If you set a rule, you follow it.

### Phase 1 — Year 1 QC, Philadelphia Eagles (January 1999 – January 2000)

**Context:** Stone is hired as Offensive Quality Control under Andy Reid's new staff in Philadelphia. He works primarily under OC Rod Dowhower, with regular interaction with Brad Childress (QB coach) and Pat Shurmur (TE coach). The Eagles go 8-8. Stone completes a full NFL coaching cycle — offseason, camp, regular season, exit meetings — for the first time.

**What Changed:**
- Stone now understands how an NFL coaching staff operates week-to-week. This was completely theoretical 12 months ago. It is now lived experience.
- He learned the Reid/WCO terminology and workflow well enough to function at weekly game-plan speed. Not instinctive yet — still translating mentally under pressure — but no longer memorizing and regurgitating.
- His film perspective shifted from player ("Where's my window?") to coach ("Why did that technique fail? How do I fix it?"). This shift is not complete, but it is real and ongoing.
- He converted his former-player identity into useful QC value without creating hierarchy problems. Staff treats him as a colleague, not a celebrity.
- He built real working trust with the coaching staff — particularly Dowhower (primary mentor, highest trust) and to a functional degree with Childress and Shurmur.

**What Didn't Change:**
- He still has zero room-ownership experience. He hasn't run a full position meeting, managed a room's attention over 30 minutes, or delivered corrections backed by positional authority.
- He hasn't recruited anyone. His recruiting knowledge is still at entry level.
- He hasn't managed other staff members.
- He hasn't called a play. He hasn't designed a play.
- His media relations as a coach are completely untested.

**Mental State (End of Year 1):**
- More confident but not overconfident. Knows what he knows. More importantly, knows what he still doesn't know.
- Understands the difference between supporting structure and creating structure — and knows he's only done the first.
- No longer feels like an outsider in the building. The novelty of "ex-player in the QC room" has worn off. He's just a QC now.
- Year 2 pressure is different: Year 1 was about proving he belongs. Year 2 is about proving he can keep growing.

**Job Status:**

- Job Security: 95/100.
- Stress: 66/100.
