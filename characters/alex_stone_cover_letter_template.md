# ALEX-LAMAR STONE — COVER LETTER

**From:** Alex-Lamar Stone, Offensive Coordinator, Arizona Cardinals
**Date:** January 15, 2012
**Re:** Head Coaching Candidacy — 2012 NFL Hiring Cycle

---

To the Hiring Committee,

I am writing to express my interest in your head coaching position. I have spent thirteen years coaching football across the NFL and college levels, and I believe my experience has prepared me to lead an NFL program.

My coaching career began in Philadelphia under Andy Reid, where I spent six years learning the West Coast Offense from quality control through tight ends coach. That stretch included two NFC Championship appearances and a Super Bowl staff experience in XXXIX. I then spent a year coaching defensive backs at Memphis before being named the program's head coach, where I took a 4-8 team to 9-4 and a Conference USA East Division title over three seasons. That role gave me complete program ownership — offense, defense, special teams, staff management, recruiting, and culture-building.

I returned to the NFL in 2009 as San Diego's passing game coordinator under Norv Turner during a 13-3 season, which deepened my fluency in the Coryell vertical passing system I had played in for twelve years. For the past two seasons, I have served as Arizona's offensive coordinator, where I have been the primary play-caller and have overseen full weekly offensive installation. In 2011, Arizona started 1-6 and finished 8-1. That turnaround was driven by in-season corrections, improved sequencing, and player buy-in — the work of coaching, not momentum.

I bring dual-system offensive mastery across the Reid West Coast and Coryell vertical trees, five years of live play-calling experience, functional defensive knowledge from coaching DBs and running both sides of the ball as a college head coach, and the credibility of a twelve-year Hall of Fame playing career. My financial position allows me to be selective. I am not looking for any job. I am looking for the right job — an organization where preparation, accountability, and structure are valued.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my background fits your program's needs. My references — Andy Reid, Rod Dowhower, Norv Turner, and Ken Whisenhunt — can speak directly to my coaching ability and character.

Respectfully,

Alex-Lamar Stone
Offensive Coordinator, Arizona Cardinals
